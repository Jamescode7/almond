Almond — first-run instructions

The first time you open Almond, macOS will warn that the app is from
an "unidentified developer". This is because v0.1.0 is not yet signed with
an Apple Developer certificate.

To bypass the warning (one time only):

  1. Drag Almond.app into your Applications folder.
  2. In Finder, right-click (or Control-click) Almond.app and
     choose "Open" from the context menu.
  3. In the dialog that appears, click "Open".

After this one-time approval, double-clicking the app will launch it
normally — macOS remembers your decision.

Why the warning?
  Almond is free and open-source. v0.1.0 ships unsigned because the
  Apple Developer Program ($99/year) is not yet registered. Code signing
  and notarization will be introduced in a later release.
